A Mindful Engineer

Background profile

Academic

I studied at Imperial College in London during four years towards a Master's degree in Biomedical Engineering with a focus on Electrical Engineering. My final year thesis was focused on the use of bioinformatics and high-throughput image analysis in an attempt to quantify the probablities of metastasis in cancer cells. The more advanced modules taken whilst at Imperial include Advanced Signal Processing and Machine Intelligence, Brain-Machine Interfaces and Computational Neuroscience.

Professional

After my first year at university, I started practicing with popular development microprocessors; Arduino, Raspberry Pi, and eventually with ESP32. The logical component to digital systems and embedded programming still remains a very interesting part of the job that I do to this day. At the beginning, I used microprocessors to retrieve information from a varied range of sensors. Once I understood the practicalities of Bluetooth and WiFi, I started using these two as ways of transferring information from the microprocessor (whilst understanding UART, SPI and I2C communications).

In my first job as an undegraduate, I was lucky to work on the back-end of website services and deal with Amazon Web Services, Dockers, and other software solutions used to close the loop in IoT and mobile applications.

Currently, I am enjoying the responsibilites and challenges in the Research and Development department of a medical device company. It's already provided me with countless learning opportunities and I continue to grow everyday, not only as a competent Software Engineer but also as a young adult. In this website, I am hoping to share discussion points and project ideas, in the hopes that others could benefit from my input.
